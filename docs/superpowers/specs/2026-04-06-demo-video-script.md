# RotateOps — 3-Minute Demo Video Script

**Format:** Polished narrated walkthrough  
**Audience:** Mixed (engineers, managers, security/compliance, portfolio viewers)  
**Goal:** Portfolio showcase  
**Approach:** Live rotation as the narrative spine  
**Target runtime:** 3:00  
**Narration pace:** ~150 words/min → ~450 words

---

## Beat-by-Beat Script

---

### [0:00–0:20] — Hook

**SCREEN:** Black / title card: "RotateOps"

> "API keys, service tokens, database credentials — every production system depends on them. But rotating them manually is slow, error-prone, and almost never done on schedule. One leaked key, one forgotten credential, and you have an incident.
>
> RotateOps is a credential rotation orchestration platform that makes rotation deterministic, auditable, and safe — automatically."

---

### [0:20–0:45] — Dashboard

**SCREEN:** Navigate to `localhost:3000/dashboard`. Seed demo data if not already loaded.

> "This is the RotateOps dashboard. At a glance, you can see all connected integrations, the number of successful rotation jobs, and anything that needs attention.
>
> The system has already been running — jobs have completed, credentials have been rotated. Let's look at how that works."

---

### [0:45–1:15] — Integrations

**SCREEN:** Navigate to `/integrations`. Hover over a row — GitHub, Stripe, or Vercel.

> "Integrations are the services RotateOps manages. Here we have GitHub, Stripe, and Vercel — each with a live status, the timestamp of their last rotation, and when the next one is scheduled.
>
> Each integration knows its own credential state: a masked reference, a fingerprint of the active secret, and whether it's healthy. Nothing raw is ever stored.
>
> You can also see the mode — demo or provider. In provider mode, RotateOps connects directly to the service API to issue and revoke real credentials."

---

### [1:15–1:50] — Triggering a Rotation Job

**SCREEN:** Click "Rotate Now" on an integration. Navigate to `/jobs` to watch status update. If possible, show the job moving through states.

> "Let's trigger a rotation now.
>
> When a job runs, it follows a strict, deterministic pipeline. First, a new credential is issued. Then it's propagated to every target that depends on it. Only after propagation is verified does the system revoke the old credential.
>
> If propagation fails, or verification fails, RotateOps automatically rolls back — the old credential is preserved, and the job is flagged for manual intervention. There is no window where both credentials are invalid.
>
> For high-risk actions — like revoking a credential tied to a live provider — the system can require step-up authentication before proceeding."

---

### [1:50–2:20] — Rotation Policies

**SCREEN:** Navigate to `/policies`. Show the interval column and the "Change Interval" control on one row.

> "Policies automate the schedule. Each integration can have a 30, 60, or 90-day rotation interval — or a custom cadence. Enable or disable policies per integration without touching the underlying configuration.
>
> When a job completes successfully, the policy automatically calculates the next run date. The scheduler picks it up from there — no cron jobs to maintain, no scripts to babysit."

---

### [2:20–2:50] — Audit Timeline

**SCREEN:** Navigate to `/audit`. Scroll slowly through events from the rotation just triggered.

> "Every rotation produces a structured audit trail. You can see exactly what happened, in what order, and when — credential issued, propagation succeeded, verification passed, old credential revoked.
>
> These events are generated by the rotation orchestrator itself, not reconstructed after the fact. That means the audit log is a reliable, tamper-evident record of every action the system took — useful for compliance, incident response, and simply knowing your infrastructure is in a known good state."

---

### [2:50–3:00] — Wrap

**SCREEN:** Return to `/dashboard`. Hold on the stats panel.

> "RotateOps: deterministic credential rotation, built-in verification, automatic rollback, and a full audit trail — so your secrets stay fresh, and your systems stay safe."
